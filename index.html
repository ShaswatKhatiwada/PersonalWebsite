Why


I am a firm believer that today’s college degree won’t land you your dream job, and that we must learn to create something that will set ourselves apart from the rest. It also takes more than a well-groomed resume for employers to notice you. If you were wondering else it might take, don’t worry! I will share my thoughts.

VCU is known for having a community for everything, from anime to GoT to tennis. I was able to find my place within the business school in the AITP. The monthly events allowed me the opportunity to network with other students as well as employers from around Virginia.


PERSONAL ACTIVITIES:

Recently I went for a job interview with a large company, and they didn’t care in the slightest what I knew or what courses I took. The truth was most of the other candidates in the position all had the same knowledge and degree as me. During they interview they asked me what I do in my free time. As someone who has been working full time since the age of 15, I wasn’t quite sure what free time meant. In my personal life, away from work, I spend my time caring for my family or improving my skills. The most enjoyable thing I’ve done recently with my free time is building this website and continuing to pursue my CompTIA Security+ and ICCP Information System Analyst certification. I’ve found that the best use of my ‘free time,’ is actually working to improve myself and learn new skills.


WORK EXPERIENCES:

Working full time is an excellent way to spend your time outside of school. Most of the advice I was given told me “not to worry about the job, it is just about getting your foot in the door.” Unfortunately, that led to me pursuing many avenues that weren’t as beneficial to me in the long run because it is better than nothing. I’ve realized this is not the approach for me. I am a very passionate and driven individual who is highly motivated to work doing what I love.
